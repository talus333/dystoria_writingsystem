The Map

Your whole world, laid out where you can see it.

Every character, faction, setting, object, concept and thread is one element you can see and connect — and that same element shows up in your plot grid, your notes and your wiki. Build it once; it stays true everywhere. For a cast of sixty, the map is your memory.

One element, every view

Each character, faction, place or object is one record — a token on the map, a cell in the plot grid, an entry in the wiki — edited once and true everywhere. Starting cold? Summon a whole cast from deep, curated word-pools.

Map every relationship

Draw a line between any two elements and name the bond, both ways. Faint until you look — then the whole web lights up. When the cast runs deep, the web is how you remember who owes what to whom.

Thread the storylines

Gather characters, stakes and events into colored threads. Highlight one and the rest recedes, so a single arc reads at a glance across a hundred scenes — and the tool flags a thread that has gone cold.

Where the AI stands

A tool for the author — not the author.

We don't believe a story should be written by a machine. The AI here is an instrument, the way a chisel or a printing press is an instrument — and the finer the instrument, the more ambitious the work a maker can bring into being.

An architect can raise a shelter with a hammer; give them sharper tools and they raise cathedrals. Dystoria's AI never writes your story for you — it clears the busywork and sharpens your sight, so the vision, the voice, and every real decision stay yours.

Keeps your world in order

Because every character, tie and thread is structured — not buried in prose — the AI actually knows your world, and keeps it straight as it grows.

Reads the patterns in your prose

Honest feedback on the shape of your writing — pacing, threads, gaps and weak points — and fresh ways to see your own text.

Breaks the blank page

Seeds, prompts and guides when you're lost or stuck — never generic, always tuned to where your story actually is.

A friend, or a critic

Talk it through with an enthusiastic reader, or invite a harsh critic — whichever you need that day.

Converse with your story

Have a conversation with any character, place or object — your answers file themselves into notes linked to that element, no organizing required.

Finds the cracks — offers ways through

Checks your prose against your own world — a trait that changed, a dead character walking, a thread left dangling — even across a whole series. You decide what is true.

Every prompt is a suggestion. Every suggestion is yours to take, bend, or throw away. You are the one telling the story — the tool just helps you tell it well.
